WINDOW CLEANER

Window cleaners are used to maintain the cleanliness of windows, safety glasses, equipment with glass panels, and more. Alcohol or ammonia-based cleaners are often preferred here due to their powerful cleaning abilities. In healthcare facilities, cleanliness is not just about appearance; it’s a matter of health and safety.


PROPER USAGE & DISPOSAL

To use glass cleaner effectively read the label for instructions, ensure good ventilation, and clear the area around the glass. Use a microfiber cloth or paper towel applying the cleaner with a light mist. Wipe in circular or straight motions to avoid streaks and follow up with a dry cloth if needed. Clean when surfaces are cool and shaded. For disposal, check local hazardous waste regulations, rinse empty containers for recycling, and take any leftover cleaner to a hazardous waste facility. Store the cleaner in a cool dry place and avoid mixing it with other products.


PRECAUTIONARY MEASURES

LorePrevent further leakage or spillage if safe to do so. Prevent spreading over a wide area (e.g. by containment or oil barriers). Aminated wash water. Local authorities should be advised if significant spillages: Non-sparking tools should be used. Soak up with inert absorbent material.
